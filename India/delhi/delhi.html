Delhi/ Motorbikes and Trains

Delhi

Smells like a hot urinal and is bustling 24 hours per day.

Bikes

Of course it had to be Royal Enfield Bullets, but the 1950s design, with the kick start only. This caused many problems, as they stalled frequently and refused to start. The technician aimed with a crowbar and a spark plug tool, changed the plug to get them going. This created an incident pit of trying to catch up the group, and rounding corners to be met by buses and lorries. This meant for me high siding and tobogganing down a steep grass slope.

Trains

India runs on trains. Express trains travelling at up to 40mph.The Indian Army train from Kalka to Shimla is one of the World's finest train journeys, apparently. People go to India just to experience it. It takes 51/2 hours to travel 100 miles, through 100 tunnels (probably not the exact times, number of tunnels and distance, but you get the idea). This is very interesting for the first hour, but 5 1/2 hours, come on!! Even the kids got fed up of screaming every time when went through a tunnel. Luckily on the return I fell asleep and so did the train on going aback after dropping us off at Kalka, as it fell off the tracks.

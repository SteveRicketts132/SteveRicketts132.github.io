Kibber and Tabo

Kibber

At 4000m and arguably one of the more remote Himalayan villages, it is famed for being the home of the snow leopard. Which we only saw stuffed abomilly in a monastery. There is always a low point on a tour and this was it. Cold and dark at night. Boiled egg curry and no beer. The rooms were only half finshed.

Tabo

Another stop on the way to somewhere. It is the site of an ancient Buddhist temple, which looks like nothing from the outside, to dissuade Muslim hoards from attacking it in the past. The inside is covered in fantastic paintings, which a monk is happy to explain to you, in perfect English.Carving on stones is interesting. Being so far from light pollution watching the stars from the roof of the hotel, is well worth it.

Shimla

This is the old Raj Summer Capital, from where the Indian Empire was ruled. There are buildings that would not be out of place in Surrey, notably a church and what appears to be a library. There are other once fine buildings, in a state of total disrepair and now home only to monkeys. It is the terminus for the little railway, which appears on tv programmes frequently. A really fascinating city to wonder through.

Glamping

Money

You cannot get Indian Rupees in the UK, even in Leicester. Apart from at the Indian Airport it is very difficult to find places to exchange sterling into Rupees. Cash machines are few and far between and hit and miss, if they will allow UK bank cards to work.Having exchanged £100 at the airport, the tour company wanted £30 kitty for fuel and a £40 kitty for lunch and chai stops. Leaving £30. Most of the other people on the tour were n the same boat. Borrowing and paying back became the norm. In Kalka, we had a day off and busied ourselves with finding money. I had a very civilized interview with a bank manager, couldn't exchange any money, but a very pleasant experience. In Manali the problem was solved by the Western Union Office. There were foot high stacks of Rupees waiting to be exchanged for Sterling.

Hue-People-Climate and Food

Hue

Hue is the former Imperial capital. Gnoc says the North Vietnamese Army captured the citadel in the Tet Offensive. The gates are scared by shell holes. Here the French influence is strongest. You can get baguettes, but don't mention the French. They seem to have upset the Vietnamese more than the Americans. It is actually nice to be from a nation less despised for once. The River is referred to as the Perfume River due to Joss sticks being burned along its bank. Dragon Boats, made from the steel that the American lined their runways with, sail up and down to the resting place of the Emperors. One of the best theme bars I have been to is the DMZ, in Hue. The seats are oil drums and the lights are shaped light bombs. They play sixties music, to add to the atmosphere. (Click the crest at the top to return to vietnam page)

Food-Tasty

From Hanoi to Hue we ate what we were given with chopsticks, no other cutlery was offered. It was mostly food that got trapped in your teeth and you spend the morning journey trying to get it out with your tongue. Having succeeded by lunchtime, when you ate lunch and spend the afternoon using your tongue again. Then there was dinner. There wasn't any food stops along the Western Ho Chi Minh Trail, until we came to a shop. In the corner was a monkey in a small cage. Gnoc was obviously waiting for a comment, when a member of the tour said something, he lunched into You western people are quick to judge us. These man is a ranger and he has recued the monkey from the villagers, He has tried to release the monkey, but it keeps coming back. The ranger's mother said something to Gnoc in Vietnamese. Gnoc translated it We let the monkey out twice a week and it shits in the kitchen We had chicken noodle soup with a duck egg in it.

Climate - Hot

Allegedly It's hot! Damn hot! Real hot! Hottest things is my shorts. I could cook things in it. But on my trip it rained from Hanoi to Tan Ky and of course in Khe San, but that just added to the depressive atmosphere of the place. Gnoc liked his motorcycles shiny, so we were sent to the jet wash. The lady operative got carried away and jet washed us. This was fine, but I have developed the minimalist approach to foreign travel meaning one pair of motorcycle jeans, 3 pairs of socks, pants and t shirts, and a down jacket. Hence our hotel was the one with the jeans hanging out the window trying to dry.

Nha Trang/ Quy Nhon and Geography

Nha Trang

Before going I was told go soon, before the American discover and wreck the country. This part of the coast is being developed for the mass Chinese and Russian holiday trade. Benidorm with extra rice! The only road is the Route 1, which is equally uninteresting. Americans , as far a I could see, stayed in the cultural parts, in Hue and Hoi an, as did the French. There is a very interesting temple complex across the river, which is worth a visit. (Click the crest at the top to return home.)

Quy Nhon

This was the beginning of the Bucket and Spade thing in Vietnam. There are fabulous beaches and the sea is as warm as a bath. It is favored by the Vietnamese themselves. Not for the first time I wondered what local people made of the foreign traveller and their needs.

Geography

You have to be struck by the emerald green fields of rice, in the valleys, bordered by the karst outcrops. This is the classic Vietnam landscape, leading down to Halong Bay, with its thousands of outcrops in a flooded bay.Rice is grown everywhere. Gnoc stated 3 billion tones per year, of which 10% is given to North Korea. Apparently there is a lot of coffee grown, but I saw very little. The French grew rubber here and this evident today.
